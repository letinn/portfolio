Wade

Screenprinted book

Wade (verb) walk with effort through water or another liquid or viscous substance. In Vietnamese, the word for water and country is the same: nuóc Wade is a accordian-fold, screenprinted book that attempts to materialize the feelings of melancholy and grief that comes with migration and the shift in understanding of one’s own concept of home. Wade serves as a diary featuring stream of consciousness writing and images pulled from personal collections and scanned from documents related to immigration including visas, passports, birth certificates, etc.
